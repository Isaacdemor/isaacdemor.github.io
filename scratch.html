Why I Fish

There's something deeply restorative about standing at the water's edge before dawn, rod in hand, waiting for that first tug. Fishing isn't just a hobby — it's a way of life that teaches patience, respect for nature, and the quiet satisfaction of self-reliance.

Whether you're chasing brown trout in an Idaho river or casting from a mountain lake at sunrise, the water calls to all of us in the same way.

Essential Fishing Gear

🪝 Must-Have Tackle

  • Spinning rod and reel combo
  • Monofilament or fluorocarbon line (8–12 lb test)
  • Assorted hooks (sizes 4–1/0)
  • Split shot sinkers and bobbers
  • Needle-nose pliers for hook removal
  • Tackle box with compartments

📋 Getting Started: Step by Step

  1. Choose your fishing location and check local regulations
  2. Purchase a fishing license for your state
  3. Select a rod appropriate for your target species
  4. Spool your reel with the correct line weight
  5. Tie on a hook using an improved clinch knot
  6. Add bait or lure and cast toward structure
  7. Wait patiently, then set the hook on a bite!

Pro Tips for Better Catches

🌅

Fish at Dawn & Dusk

Fish are most active during low-light periods. Early morning and evening produce the best results for most species.

🌡️

Follow the Temperature

Water temperature dictates fish behavior. Bass prefer 65–75°F; trout like cooler 50–60°F water.

🗺️

Find the Structure

Fish congregate near rocks, fallen trees, weed beds, and drop-offs. Structure = shelter = fish.
